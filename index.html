Our Mission
Founded on 24 October 1961 by Mr Liu Hing-Chai, the Astronomers' Club has operated for over half a century. We are dedicated to not only promoting astronomy among our brethren but also forging bonds between them. Our mission has brought many together, evocative of the brotherhood we hold so dear.
Our Activities
Astronomers' Night is regular astronomy talk and stargazing-in-one activity held twice a month. The Astronomers' Nights give participants a chance to know more about the Space above and a rare, hands-on experience to operate our equipment. We also organise solar observations and off-campus stargazing.
Our Rooftop
The signature rooftop dome only constitutes one part of the Club's equipment on campus. Another dedicated room allows us access to the rooftop, storage for telescopes, and a venue reserved for Club activities.
Our Facilities
The Stanley Ho Astronomical Observatory, opened in 2019, is an off-campus site situated in Tai Tam. The Observatory benefits from lesser light pollution and a more isolated locale compared to the campus.
Our Activities
The Observatory houses high-end equipment exclusive to Club members, affording us to make long-term observations and astrophotography opportunities.
Our Facilities
The isolated location gives way to opportunities previously impossible on-campus, while also allowing observation in the relative comfort and protection of the Observatory.
